APPLICATION TO PUP

I chose PUP because it offers quality education that is accessible and affordable. As a state university, it provides opportunities for students like me to pursue higher education without the heavy financial burden. PUP also has a strong reputation for producing competent and socially aware graduates, which aligns with my personal values and goals. I admire the university’s commitment to public service, academic excellence, and student empowerment.

ENROLLMENT

Passing and enrolling at PUP wasn’t just a personal victory—it was a turning point. In a country where access to quality education often feels like a privilege, earning my spot at PUP reminded me that dreams are valid, even when the odds feel stacked against you. It wasn’t just about passing an entrance exam—it was about proving to myself that I am capable, that I belong, and that my future is worth fighting for. As I walked through the gates of the university for the first time, I didn’t just feel happiness—I felt purpose. I’m not just here to study; I’m here to grow, to serve, and to become part of something bigger than myself.

Not easy. Not instant.

This is the truth of an Iska

This portfolio is not just a compilation of accomplishments—it is a map of survival, surrender, and self-discovery. It tells the story of someone who once believed they were a big fish in a small pond—until the gates of PUP opened, and the waters widened.

When I stepped into the Polytechnic University of the Philippines, I carried the confidence of someone who had always done well, who was often praised, who believed she was ready. But PUP was not a pond—it was an ocean. And in that ocean swam minds so sharp, spirits so resilient, and stories so profound that I suddenly questioned my place in it all. I was no longer the big fish. I was barely swimming.

It’s humbling to meet brilliance. It’s painful to realize you’re not the smartest in the room. But it’s also freeing. Because in that space of doubt, I began to rebuild—not to compete, but to contribute. I learned to anchor my worth not in how I compared, but in how I continued.

Balancing work and study isn’t poetic. It’s exhausting. It means deadlines in the middle of shifts, lectures in the middle of sleep, and tears no one sees. There were days when I thought, If I just had more—more time, more money, more rest—how far could I go? I don’t know the answer. But I do know that even with less, I kept going.

I chose PUP not because it was easy, but because it was honest. It reflected the kind of teacher I want to become: grounded, resourceful, and in touch with reality. I didn’t choose teaching because it was glamorous. I chose it because I believe in its power to heal, to shape, to liberate. And even though my current job offers better pay, quicker success, and a clearer path, something in me aches for the classroom. For the chance to stand before students who are just as lost, just as hopeful, just as brave as I was.

My greatest dilemma is choosing between stability and significance. But I remind myself: I did not come this far to live a life that doesn’t align with my purpose.
So here I am—still swimming. Not the biggest fish. Not the fastest. But perhaps the one who refuses to stop, even when the tide rises.


This is not a success story.
This is a becoming.
The truth of an ordinary Iska.
